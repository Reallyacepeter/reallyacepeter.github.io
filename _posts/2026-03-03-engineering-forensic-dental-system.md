# Redefining Forensic Workflows through Code

In the intersection of medical expertise and software engineering lies a powerful opportunity to solve critical real-world problems. My current project involves developing a **Centralized Dental Record & Identification System** in collaboration with the **National Forensic Service (NFS)**.

## The Problem: Legacy and Fragmentation
Forensic dental identification is a race against time. However, fragmented paper records and inconsistent data formats often slow down the process of matching unidentified remains with existing dental history. The need for a robust, cross-platform digital solution has never been more urgent.

## The Solution: Why Flutter?
For this project, I chose **Flutter** as the primary framework. The decision was driven by three technical requirements:
1. **Cross-Platform Consistency:** Forensic experts need access across mobile and desktop environments without UI discrepancies.
2. **Rapid Prototyping:** The ability to iterate on complex data entry forms for dental charting.
3. **Robust State Management:** Handling intricate dental data models effectively.

## Technical Architecture
The system is designed with a focus on **data integrity and search efficiency**. By leveraging a centralized database and building a structured schema for dental characteristics, we are moving away from manual comparisons toward a more automated, logic-driven matching process.

* **Frontend:** Flutter (Dart)
* **Logic:** Structured Java-based data processing
* **Focus:** UX optimization for high-stress forensic environments

## Impact and Future Path
This app is more than just a tool for record-keeping; it is a foundation for **Automated Identification**. As I refine the architecture, the next logical step is integrating **Computer Vision** to automate the matching of radiographic images—a challenge that leads me directly into the realm of **Artificial Intelligence research**.
